# Synthetic Episode Workflow

## Complete Workflow: Generating a 23-Minute TV Episode in 4 Days (10 Steps)

### 1. Character Creation with Higgsfield Soul Cast

Develop every character using Higgsfield Soul Cast. Create highly detailed character sheets with front/back views, close-ups, specific props (e.g., skateboards), and a huge variety of emotional/physical states to maintain consistency across all generations.

### 2. Mastering the Master Location

Generate a single "master" image of the central location (e.g., museum). Use Nano Banana Pro to spin the camera and create five distinct angles (including difficult back views). Stitch them into a collage for reference. Create three versions: pristine, destroyed, and dark/moody for different story moments.

### 3. Technical Pre-vis with Blender Blocking & Geospatial Mapping

Build low-poly versions of all locations in Blender. Use simple colored shapes (e.g., pink for one character, brown for the monster) as a spatial map to precisely plan where characters stand and move in 3D space before generating.

### 4. Prompt Architecture with Claude

Upload the Blender spatial maps and storyboards to Claude. Have it generate precise, context-first prompts tailored for Higgsfield Seedance 2.0 based on the script.

### 5. Production Phase: Scene Coverage in Higgsfield Seedance 2.0

Generate the bulk of the animation in 15-second segments. Use a "context-first" prompting method (first establish environment and positioning, then extreme close-ups for dialogue). Generate high volume and pick the best 3-4 cuts out of every 20 to keep action intentional.

### 6. Dialogue and Pacing

Intentionally add pacing and pauses between lines of dialogue in every prompt. This prevents the AI from rushing performances and creates realistic, cinematic lip-sync and acting.

### 7. Voice Consistency

Include a short one-sentence voice description in EVERY prompt (e.g., "Deep Japanese voice" or "25-year-old American accent"). For non-human characters, iterate random generations until the right persona appears.

### 8. Post-Production & Stitching: Master Edit Workflow

Assign scenes to multiple directors working simultaneously. Each director builds their sequences locally, then exports XML files to a lead "Director of Editing" who assembles the master file. This decentralized method allows rapid generation of 20+ minutes of content.

### 9. Final Polish and Color Grading

Import everything into DaVinci Resolve. Color-match the AI footage, then add film-like post-processing: grain, halation, and glow to soften digital edges and give it a high-end cinematic look. Avoid heavy VFX; focus on photographic textures.

### 10. 4-Day Sprint Timeline Management

Complete the script in roughly one week, then run a full 4-day sprint for all generation and editing. A small team of five directors working full-time can produce thousands of clips and deliver a finished 20-23 minute episode.
