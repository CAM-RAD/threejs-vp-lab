# Reference: Bitcoin: Killing Satoshi — Gray Box Production

## Production Details

- **Title:** Bitcoin: Killing Satoshi
- **Director:** Doug Liman (Bourne Identity, Edge of Tomorrow)
- **Cast:** Casey Affleck, Gal Gadot, Pete Davidson, Isla Fisher
- **DP:** Henry Braham BSC (Superman, Guardians of the Galaxy Vol. 3)
- **Budget:** $70M (estimated $300M traditionally — 77% reduction)
- **Producer:** Acme AI & FX (Ryan Kavanaugh, Matt Kavanaugh, Garrett Grant, Lawrence Grey)
- **Stage:** converted car showroom in West London
- **Shoot:** 20 days
- **Post:** 30 weeks, 55 AI artists
- **Locations:** ~200 distinct settings (Antarctica to Las Vegas to Antigua), all AI-generated
- **Status:** shopping at Cannes May 2026 for distribution

## The Gray Box

- Walls wrapped in gray (not green, not blue). specifically tested green and blue, found gray produced better results for their AI pipeline.
- Tracking markers (X's) on all gray surfaces for camera position extraction.
- Flat overhead lighting only. no dramatic on-set lighting. final lighting applied in post by AI.
- Physical proxy set pieces: stairs, platforms, basic architectural elements actors interact with.

## What Was Physical vs AI

- **Physical:** actors, costumes, props, proxy sets, wardrobe
- **AI-generated:** all backgrounds, all environments, all final lighting, all set extensions
- **NOT altered:** actor performances captured traditionally

## AI System

- Proprietary system by Acme AI & FX
- NOT off-the-shelf tools (not Midjourney, Sora, Runway)
- Inputs: concept art, animatics, reference footage from department heads
- Outputs refined by 55 human AI artists
- Doug Liman's company 30 Ninjas also involved

## Why Gray Instead of Green

Their AI pipeline doesn't use traditional chroma keying. The AI segmentation system uses the tracking markers and neutral gray tone as a reference surface. Gray preserves natural skin tones better and works better with modern AI segmentation tools.

## Why Not LED Volume

- Way cheaper (no LED panels, no Unreal operators, no real-time rendering)
- Way faster setup
- Unlimited flexibility in post (environments can be changed entirely after the shoot)
- Tradeoff: no in-camera lighting, reflections, or creative feedback on set

## Industry Reaction

Mixed. Praised for cost innovation. Criticized for potential job displacement of VFX workers. UK union Equity warned about performer consent issues. SAG-AFTRA watching closely. Cannes reception expected to set a de facto standard for commercial use of generative production tools.
